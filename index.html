Nadtochiy Photography — Toronto Wedding Photographer

Fine art and documentary wedding & family photographers based in Toronto. Capturing authentic, emotional, and cinematic stories across Ontario and worldwide.

About Us

Nadtochiy Photography is a husband-and-wife team — Alexandra and Vladimir Nadtochiy — specializing in fine art and documentary photography for weddings, engagements, families, and cinematic videography. Since 2008, we have captured over 500 weddings and nearly 1,000 love stories, working across Canada, Europe, and beyond.

After 11 years living and working in Montenegro, we now call Toronto home. Our mission is to document real emotions with a timeless aesthetic — natural light, elegant tones, and a sense of calm storytelling. Every frame we create aims to preserve the beauty and sincerity of human connection.

We believe that photography is not just about perfect poses but about truth — the quiet smiles, fleeting gestures, and unspoken warmth that make every story unique. Whether it’s a wedding in downtown Toronto, a lakeside engagement, or a Sunday morning with your family, our goal is always the same: to tell your story honestly and beautifully.

Wedding Photography

Our approach to wedding photography in Toronto is cinematic and emotional. We document the day naturally — from quiet morning preparations to the energy of the dance floor. With more than 15 years of experience, we’ve developed a signature blend of documentary storytelling and fine art composition.

We photograph weddings across Toronto, the GTA, Caledon, Niagara, Oakville, and beyond. Each image is carefully edited in our signature warm tones that blend film-inspired color with a modern fine-art touch. Visit our wedding galleries to explore full stories.

Engagement Photography

An engagement session is a perfect way to celebrate your story before the wedding. We design each session around your relationship — casual, intimate, and full of personality. From golden light at the lake to cozy city strolls, our engagement photos focus on authentic emotion and effortless beauty.

We love shooting in locations that feel personal — maybe where you first met, a park where you walk together, or a scenic view that represents your love. Our engagement galleries showcase couples who wanted something meaningful and timeless.

Family Photography

Our family photography is about genuine emotion, not posing. We photograph your family as you are — laughing, hugging, playing, connecting. Whether it’s at home, in a Toronto park, or by the lake, these sessions tell the story of love across generations.

Each session is calm and relaxed — we guide you gently while letting your moments unfold naturally. Our editing preserves the warmth of light and the authenticity of color, so your photos always feel true to life.

Videography

Our wedding videography transforms your memories into motion. We film cinematic wedding stories using natural light, real sound, and emotional pacing — turning your day into a film that feels like art.

We blend fine-art visuals with documentary realism, creating films that look beautiful and feel genuine. Every sound, laugh, and tear becomes part of your timeless story.

Watch our wedding film on YouTube to see how we combine cinematic direction with true documentary emotion.

Why Couples Choose Nadtochiy Photography

We’ve spent more than 15 years working with couples and families around the world. Our clients often describe our work as “calm, emotional, and cinematic.” We combine artistic direction with genuine kindness — guiding you gently while allowing natural emotion to unfold.

Every wedding or family shoot is personal. We believe in connection: we learn your story, listen carefully, and translate your personality into timeless imagery. Our couples often say they forget about the camera — and that’s when real magic happens.

We edit every image ourselves, ensuring consistent tone and detail. We use Sony cameras for digital precision and Canon EOS 1v for film warmth, blending both seamlessly for editorial yet heartfelt results.
